Hangul

Korean written language

Before I talk about the courses, I want to talk about Hangul, Korean written language system, as it provides a unique perspective on designing letterforms.

Hangul was invented by King Sejong in 1443.

Five basic consonants are designed after speech organs

'ieung' is silent at the initial position. It's mostly used with vowels that follow.

g, k

'giyeok' resembles the shape of the throat and the tongue when saying /g/ sound.

n

'nieun' resembles the shape of the tongue when saying /n/ sound.

m

'mieum' resembles the shape of the mouth (front) when saying /m/ sound.

s

'siot' resembles the shape of the tooth when saying /s/ sound.

-ng

'ieung' resembles the shape of the throat. It is used with vowels.

Other consonants

Other consonants are created by adding strokes to the basic shapes based on how strong the sound is.

Think of a turtle at the center of paper.

You will create instructions for it to follow and move.

When the turtle moves, it leaves a trace.

I will give you a shape to draw.

Discuss as a group to decide what instructions/commands to use. Keep the instructions simple - words and numbers.

Half the group will give instructions to the other half who will execute them.

  • Parameterize
  • Function
  • Modularize
  • Iteration
1. Instead of saying 'draw a BIG square,' we can parameterize to respond to different conditons.
2. The way we command the turtle, different actions it can take is a function.
3. Instructions are simple; We break them into smaller pieces.
4. We repeat and improve the logic every time
When we have a set of simple instructions such as forward, left, right, we can combine them to create a more complex, reusable instructions. Again, they become a function.
